    Relevant Courses

    Introduction to Blockchain Technologies

    Insead The Business School,
    Fontainebleau (Via Coursera)

    This course provides a brief introduction about Blockchain technologies. Blockchain technology is a revolutionary and decentralized system that enables secure, transparent, and tamper-resistant recording of transactions. It was initially developed as the underlying technology for cryptocurrencies like Bitcoin but has since found applications in various industries beyond finance.

    Smart Contracts

    The State University of New York,
    Buffalo (Via Coursera)

    This course was developed to help design, code, deploy and execute a Smart contract – the computational element of the Blockchain technology. The Capstone project consisted of designing and programming Smart contracts in Solidity language, testing and deploying them in the Remix development environment. This course features best practices for designing solutions with Smart contracts using Solidity and Remix IDE.

    Decentralized Applications (Dapps)

    The State University of New York,
    Buffalo (Via Coursera)

    This course prepares you to design and develop end-to-end Dapps which provide anyone with access to the Blockchain’s features and services. The course covers the basic design of a Dapp, Truffle development process and commands (init, develop, test and migrate), test-driven development of Dapp, Dapp application models and emerging standards that are essential for predictable Dapp behavior.

    Decentralized Finance (DeFi) Infrastructure

    Duke University,
    North Carolina (Via Coursera)

    This course primarily focussed on Decentralized Finance, commonly known as DeFi, represents a revolutionary shift in the world of finance. DeFi refers to a blockchain-based ecosystem of financial applications and services that operate without traditional intermediaries like banks, brokers, or insurance companies. Instead, DeFi relies on smart contracts and decentralized networks to facilitate and automate financial transactions and services.
